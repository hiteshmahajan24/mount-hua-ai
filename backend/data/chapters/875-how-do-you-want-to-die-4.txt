Wei So-Heng supported Wei Lishan, who was staggering as if he might collapse at any moment.

He would have liked to lay him down on the ground to rest, but this was a battlefield. For the sake of the Hwayoung clan disciples, who were desperately holding on with trembling legs, Wei Lishan couldn’t collapse.

“Father. He is here… Taoist…”

Even in Wei Lishan’s voice, there was undeniable relief.

The situation hadn’t changed much.

The fangs of the wolves targeting them were sharp, and those who hadn’t yet fully filled their stomachs would once again come to bite them.

But even so, Wei Lishan was no longer worried.

That was the only way.

Anyone who knew the name of the person standing in front of him, who understood what kind of person he was, would feel the same way as Wei Lishan.

Standing on the edge of a cliff, Xi’an warriors who had been fighting with their lives at stake bit their lips when they saw this light standing in front of them.

“… Taoist Chung Myung.”

It was then that someone’s muttering made them realize it again.

“… Mount Hua has come.”

The echo of those words swept over those who endured the fight until then.

Just a few years ago, the name of Mount Hua had no meaning in Xi’an.

But now, it was entirely different.

Beyond Xi’an, in Shaanxi, the meaning of Mount Hua had become so great that nothing could replace it.

Those who were terrified of death, those who were panting because they didn’t have the strength to lift their swords, and those who desperately dragged their legs to escape—all began to burn with fighting spirit again the moment they heard the name, Mount Hua.

With just three people appearing for them.

Gal Cheon-rip, who was watching this, clicked his tongue.

The situation had changed so much. The fact that the warriors of Xi’an were so tenaciously clinging to them could have been dismissed as nothing more than tantrums.

But it wasn’t like that. The atmosphere had changed because of the three people.

The bigger problem was that those who were running around like beasts were now holding their breath.

If this force waned any further, his plan might go up in smoke.

Gal Cheon-rip looked at the person standing in front of him with an uncomfortable look.

A man in a black robe with a plum blossom engraved on it was looking at them with his hands hanging down. The moment he faced the coldness in the eyes, a name came to his mind naturally.

A name so famous, but whose luster had now faded with the passage of time.

Gal Cheon-rip opened his mouth, looking straight at the man.

“Are you Mount Hua’s Sword Saint?”

Mount Hua’s Sword Saint.

The moment that name was mentioned, the entire Evil Sect reacted strongly.

“Mount Hua’s Sword Saint?”

“The… Yangtze River…”

The faces of the Evil Sect people turned pale.

They couldn’t even finish their words, but it was clear what they were talking about.

He had fought on equal terms with Jang Ilso.

Everyone here fled to avoid that man. There was no need to explain how much weight Jang Ilso, the leader of the alliance, held over them.

The leader of the Four Precepts Alliance, the undisputed hegemon.

The warrior who turned the South of the river into a land of blood and finally achieved his unification.

At one time, the title of one of the Shenzhou Five Sects was attached to his name. Still, now, three years after the Yangtze River incident, no one in the world called Jang Ilso with such a simple title.

So naturally, the name of Mount Hua’s Sword Saint couldn’t help but shine. In recent years, the only one who tarnished the name of Jang Ilso was Mount Hua’s Sword Saint.

A tense atmosphere filled the room, but the man simply stared at Gal Cheon-rip, who had opened his mouth without saying a word.

“I guess I was right.”

When Chung Myung didn’t respond, Gal Cheon-rip laughed softly.

“This kid has become quite arrogant after gaining fame. Bastard. Would anyone believe that you are a warrior on par with Jang Ilso? I didn’t believe it before, but seeing you with my own eyes makes me more certain of it.”

At those words, the members of the Evil Sect stared at Chung Myung with wary eyes.

“Why? Do you have nothing to say?”

At that moment, Chung Myung, who had been silent, snickered and spoke.

“What is ridiculous? You are supposed to be as good as Jang Ilso…”

Gal Cheon-rip complained, but Chung Myung cut him off.

“A fucking moron from the Evil Sect is shaking his butt in front of me.”

The smile vanished from Gal Cheon-rip’s face.

Chung Myung tilted his neck to the left and right as if he had no more intention of listening to the man.

“If the Evil Sect is going to show up in front of me, then okay. One has to be at least on the level of that moron Jang Ilso like you said. I can at least tolerate what that idiot has to say. But…”

A sneer appeared on Chung Myung’s face.

“Not you. You little bastard.”

“So shut your mouth. I have no interest in talking to those who will die.”

Gal Cheon-rip’s face contorted.

When in his life had he ever been called such?

What made him even angrier was that this little brat was treating him as lower than Jang Ilso. This was a sore spot for Gal Cheon-rip.

He clenched his fist and suppressed his anger, so furious that his hair was standing up. To show anger in front of all these people would be the same as admitting he was caught in the words of this kid.

He gritted his teeth and then spoke, trying his best to appear calm.

“That son of a bitch has a sharp tongue, I see. But you should keep your boasting in check. Even if you had the rumored skills, three people are nothing…”

“I said your fucking mouth stinks, so shut it.”

But this time, a cold voice cut him off again. Gal Cheon-rip’s face hardened to the point where he couldn’t remain calm.

“This little brat is messing around…”

“You seem to be mistaken.”

Chung Myung bared his teeth.

“I am holding back from wanting to tear you to pieces right now. So do not open your mouth. My patience has a limit.”

The eyes of Dam Hae, the youngest, were red with anger.

However, Gal Cheon-rip felt more shock than anger at Chung Myung’s words.

“You are holding back?”

“Why? What are you holding back for?”

Chung Myung said with a sneer.

“I am not the one who is going to catch and kill you.”

“And let me correct another misunderstanding.”

Chung Myung snorted and asked.

“Who said we were just three?”

“It surely isn’t just three.”

Gal Cheon-rip hurriedly turned around at the clear voice behind him.

A handsome young man in a black robe was slowly walking past the gate of the merchant union.

The moment he saw him, Gal Cheon-rip’s body was unconsciously filled with tension.

The steps were neither too much nor too little. His breathing was neither fast nor slow. All of it indicated that this man had reached a level of controlling his body.

His angry gaze lingered on Gal Cheon-rip’s face for a moment.

“It isn’t that there are just three around.”

As soon as he said so, a group of warriors appeared behind him.

‘W-when? I couldn’t feel any movement?’

But that wasn’t all.

One by one, people in black robes climbed up the extremely tall walls around them.

The eyes of the Evil Sect people who saw this trembled as if they had experienced something unknown.

Were they surrounded?

Or was it because an unexpected enemy appeared?

No, the reason they were panicking was the sharp force of those who surrounded them.

‘Just where did these people come from…?’

It wasn’t the heavy and gentle pressure of the Justice faction. It felt as if they would cut themselves if they touched anything… no, just meeting them felt like blades piercing their hearts, pressuring them.

In particular, what caught their attention was the woman standing in the center of those on the wall.

And a cold, cold killing intent that covered even that beauty.

Those who faced such gazes, which held no trace of emotion, stepped back.

The killing intent they exuded was like a hundred sword ghosts standing around them.

The more skilled one was, the greater the pressure. Only those with experience dealing with such killing intent could understand how formidable they were.

Cold sweat ran down Dam Hae’s forehead.

There were hundreds of those people…

‘Damn it. What is happening?’

Just looking at the plum blossom pattern engraved on their chests, it was clear.

A famous sword sect from Shaanxi that once aimed for the title of the Best Sword Sect in the world.

But the current Mount Hua was by no means a famous sect. Didn’t everyone in the world know that the fame gained by Mount Hua wasn’t its own, but rather by the works of Mount Hua’s Sword Saint?

But when Mount Hua actually encountered them, they shattered such thoughts from Dam Hae.

If he could, he would find all the people who said that Mount Hua itself was nothing special and slit their mouths.

But regret always came too late, and when he realized how wrong he was, it was after everything was done.

The vast expanse of Eunha’s mansion began to feel narrow now.

At that moment, those who had entered through the main gate of the manor and occupied the area behind them split to the left and right. Three old men walked between them.

A scream, like a shout, burst from the mouths of the Hwayoung disciples.

The main sect leader of Mount Hua, Hyun Jong, walked in with a cold, hard look on his face as he surveyed the yard.

A rare anger welled up in the eyes of Hyun Jong as he saw the miserable corpses cooling down and the survivors who were still breathing, covered in blood.

He clenched his fists and gritted his teeth as he shouted.

“Un Geom. Baek Cheon!”

The two people whose names were called out immediately appeared.

The force that Hyun Jong exuded was tremendous. It wasn’t as intense as the killing intent of his disciples, but it was surely a mountain of wrath.

Even without the famous title of the sect leader of Mount Hua, no one would be able to ignore Hyun Jong now if they saw him.

“You dare invade Shaanxi.”

The moment the heavy voice of Hyun Jong fell, groans of pain escaped from the mouths of the Evil Sect people.

“Attack close friends of Mount Hua!”

Upon hearing those words, the disciples of the Hwayoung Sect and the Xi’an sects clenched their fists.

“IN this land of MOUNT HUA….”

After catching his breath, Hyun Jong firmly ordered.

“Make those who dare commit such heinous acts pay the price for what they did! Show them clearly what the wrath of Mount Hua is!”

“We accept the order!”

As soon as the words fell, the disciples of Mount Hua drew their swords at once.

The sight of a hundred swordsmen, seemingly devoid of any emotion, drawing their swords at once, brought shivers down the people.

To those who had been waiting for them, it was an indescribable thrill and overwhelming feeling.

To those who had to handle them, it was suffocating pressure and fear.

“In the name of Mount Hua!”

A majestic voice burst from the mouth of Baek Cheon.

“Punish the evildoers!”

The disciples of Mount Hua, with their swords extended downwards, rushed at the Evil Sect people simultaneously. It was like a black wave rippling in the middle of Xi’an.

The movement declared to the world that Mount Hua had finally returned.
